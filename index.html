What I Enjoy the Most?

I'm interested in how the brain regulates food-related decisions and its role in obesity. I study how lifestyle and dietary interventions might influence food choices and the gut-brain axis, aiming to inform strategies for healthier weight management.

My work bridges cognitive neuroscience and clinical psychology, combining experimental and clinical perspectives.

Training/Book

My training is grounded in Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), enriched by a neuroscience-informed perspective. The focus is on understanding how thoughts, emotions, and behaviors interact at both psychological and neural levels.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy

CBT is a structured, evidence-based approach that helps individuals identify and modify unhelpful thought patterns and behaviors. In my work, CBT principles are applied to enhance self-awareness, improve emotional regulation, and support sustainable behavioral change.

Brain Obesity

This book translates neuroscience research into practical strategies for understanding eating behavior and obesity, integrating CBT-based strategies.
